1. Scope & Purpose of Processing

FBF SSD A R.L. (“FBF”, “we”, “us”) processes personal data to operate a sports talent environment, including talent discovery, sports career opportunities, networking, athlete profile management, communications related to sporting activities and events, and connected services strictly necessary to run the platform and club operations.

2. Legal Bases (Art. 6 GDPR)

Depending on the context, processing relies on one or more of the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): account creation, profile management, access to platform features.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): platform security, fraud prevention, service improvements, internal reporting (balanced against your rights).
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): optional features (e.g., visibility options, certain communications). You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legal obligations (Art. 6(1)(c)): compliance with applicable laws and regulatory requests.

We do not request special category data (Art. 9) by default. If you voluntarily share sensitive data (e.g., health-related information), we will process it only where strictly necessary and with an appropriate legal basis (e.g., explicit consent).

3. Categories of Personal Data

  • Identification data: name, nationality, date of birth.
  • Contact details: email address, phone number, city/country of residence.
  • Sports & professional data: sport, role/position, career history, achievements, links to highlights/media you provide.
  • Technical data: device information, IP address, log data necessary for security and service delivery.
  • Preferences & visibility: profile settings, communication preferences.

4. Sources of Data

Data is obtained primarily from you during registration and profile completion. We may also receive technical/usage data generated by your interaction with the platform. Any third-party media links you add (e.g., external video URLs) remain under your control and may have their own privacy policies.

5. Registration of Minors under 14

Users under the age of 14 may register and use the platform only with the explicit consent and ongoing supervision of a parent or legal guardian. Where required, we implement parental consent verification. Without valid parental consent, the account cannot be created or activated.

Parents/guardians may request access, correction, or deletion of the minor’s data at any time using the contacts below. If we become aware that a minor under 14 has registered without valid consent, we will take steps to suspend or delete the account promptly.

6. Recipients & Sharing

We do not share your personal data with third parties without your explicit consent unless required by law or strictly necessary to provide the platform and club services (e.g., IT service providers acting as processors). When we use processors, we put in place data processing agreements and require appropriate security measures.

7. International Transfers

Personal data is primarily processed and stored within the European Union. If, exceptionally, data is transferred outside the EEA, we will ensure appropriate safeguards (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses) and conduct transfer risk assessments where required.

8. Storage & Retention

We retain personal data for as long as your account remains active and for a reasonable period thereafter to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Where the law prescribes specific retention periods, we retain data accordingly and delete or anonymize it once the period expires.

9. Security Measures

We apply appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data, including access controls, encryption in transit where applicable, role-based permissions, monitoring, back-ups, and staff confidentiality commitments. While no method is entirely risk-free, we continuously review and improve our safeguards.

10. Data Subject Rights

You have the following rights under GDPR, subject to conditions and applicable law:

  • Access – obtain a copy of your personal data we process.
  • Rectification – correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure – request deletion (“right to be forgotten”) in qualifying cases.
  • Restriction – limit processing in certain circumstances.
  • Portability – receive data in a structured, commonly used format and transmit it to another controller.
  • Objection – object to processing based on legitimate interests or to direct marketing.
  • Withdrawal of consent – when processing relies on consent, you may withdraw it at any time.

Exercising these rights may affect access to certain features that rely on the relevant processing.

11. Automated Decision-Making / Profiling

We do not make decisions producing legal effects solely based on automated processing, including profiling, within the meaning of Art. 22 GDPR. If we introduce any automated scoring or ranking features, we will provide clear information, safeguards, and options to contest decisions or request human review.
